Sprint 25 batch 5 — B9 mass-assignment hardening
=================================================

Audit context:
  - Pre-sweep: 0 Pydantic schemas in the entire codebase used extra="forbid"
  - Pydantic v2 default behavior = silently ignore extra fields
  - Mass-assignment risk depends on whether handlers do **payload.dict()
    into an ORM. Found only ONE such site: services/usage/repository/usage.py:36
    (UsageRecord insert, not a security-sensitive entity).
  - Real exposure for security-facing POST endpoints is therefore LOW today,
    but defense-in-depth + better error UX (422 instead of silent drop) is cheap.

Schemas hardened:
  - services/registry/schemas.py::AgentCreate         (POST /agents body)
  - services/registry/schemas.py::PermissionCreate    (POST /agents/{id}/permissions body)
  - services/policy/router.py::PolicyTestRequest      (POST /policies/test body)

Verification (Pydantic round-trip):
  - AgentCreate with extra `is_admin=True`         → ValidationError ✓
  - PermissionCreate with extra `is_admin=True`    → ValidationError ✓
  - PolicyTestRequest with extra `is_admin=True`   → ValidationError ✓

Intentionally NOT hardened (deferred to sprint-26):
  - Internal-only schemas (EvaluationRequest, SimulateRequest) — gated by
    verify_internal_secret, no external attack surface.
  - services/usage/repository/usage.py:36 — UsageRecord.values(**payload.model_dump())
    pattern. UsageRecord is a billing usage row; extras would hit the SQLAlchemy
    column-not-found error path, not become writeable columns. Flag for verify.
  - The other ~14 BaseModel POST schemas across identity/, audit/, identity_graph/.
    Ponytail rule: don't expand the ticket beyond what was asked. Sprint-26
    sweep can apply ConfigDict(extra="forbid") project-wide.
